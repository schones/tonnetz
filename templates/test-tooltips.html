Inline Terms

Music begins with sound — vibrations that we organize into pitch using note names and notate on the staff. The distance between any two pitches is called an interval, and when you stack intervals in thirds you get triads — the building blocks of harmony. These relationships wrap elegantly around the Circle of Fifths, which maps every key to its neighbors.

Advanced Concepts

The PLR transforms let you morph between triads by moving a single note. These operations form the basis of neo-Riemannian theory, which explains the lush chromatic chord progressions found in film scores and late Romantic music.
