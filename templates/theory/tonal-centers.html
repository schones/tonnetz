What is a tonal center?

The tonal center — also called the tonic — is the note that functions as home base in a piece of music. Everything else gravitates toward it.

It's not about one note being "prettier" than others. It's about relationship: the tonic is the note that resolves tension and makes the music feel finished.

Key = tonic + diatonic family

When you choose a tonal center, you get a family of seven chords that naturally belong together — the diatonic chords. The Circle of Fifths shows this as a sliding window: the highlighted arc is your key's chord family.

Adjacent keys on the circle share six of seven chords. Keys across the circle share almost none. That's why modulating to a nearby key sounds smooth, and to a distant one sounds dramatic.
